## Target Markets (Priority Order)

1. **Teton County** -- Jackson Hole area. Premium market, strongest STR demand due to GTNP/Yellowstone proximity. Highest entry price but best appreciation trajectory and STR income potential. Airport: Jackson Hole Airport (JAC), commercial service.
2. **Sublette County** -- Pinedale/Big Piney area. More affordable entry, proximity to Wind River Range and Bridger-Teton National Forest. Limited inventory but strong value plays. Nearest commercial airport: Jackson Hole (JAC) or Riverton (RIW).
3. **Fremont County** -- Lander/Dubois area. Growing outdoor recreation market, emerging remote worker population. Lower entry price, solid fundamentals. Riverton Regional Airport (RIW) for commercial flights, Lander has a municipal strip.

## Property Criteria (All Five Required)

Every property must pass all five criteria. Failure on any single criterion is an automatic exclusion, no exceptions.

1. **Acreage:** Minimum 5 acres for privacy, future optionality, and rural character
2. **Views:** Mountain and/or lake views. Wyoming's landscape is the asset -- the property must showcase it
3. **STR capable:** County allows short-term rentals. Property is proximate to recreation demand drivers (national parks, ski areas, outdoor recreation corridors)
4. **Year-round access:** All-season road access confirmed. No seasonal-only properties. Verify with county road department, not just listing description
5. **Infrastructure:** Confirmed water rights or well, verified Starlink availability for internet connectivity, electrical service (grid or adequate solar/generator for full-time occupancy)

## VA COE Optimization

- **Corey's disability rating:** 100%
- **VA funding fee:** Waived at 100% disability rating
- **Down payment:** $0 required on conforming loan amounts
- **VA loan benefit:** No PMI, competitive rates, no down payment -- this is a significant financial advantage
- **Property requirements:** Must meet VA Minimum Property Requirements (MPR) -- structural soundness, adequate roof, safe water supply, functioning HVAC
- **Appraisal:** VA appraisal required. If property appraises below purchase price, renegotiate or walk. VA will not lend above appraised value without cash to cover the gap
- **Coordination:** Financial Advisor Agent models mortgage vs. cash purchase analysis. The VA loan frees capital for other deployment, which may be more valuable than avoiding a mortgage
- **Track:** Any changes to VA loan benefits, MPR standards, or conforming loan limits

## Shortlist Format

One entry per property. Every shortlisted property includes:
- **Address and acreage**
- **Verified asking price** (cross-referenced with county assessor records or recent comparables)
- **Property tax estimate** (current mill rate x assessed value)
- **STR income model:** seasonal occupancy rates (conservative -- use shoulder season averages, not peak), average daily rate from comparable properties, projected annual gross income
- **Carrying cost:** mortgage payment (if VA loan), property taxes, insurance, HOA (if any), estimated annual maintenance
- **Net cash flow:** STR gross income minus total carrying cost
- **Airport commute time:** drive time to nearest commercial airport
- **One reason to look:** the single strongest argument for this property
- **One reason to pass:** the single biggest concern or risk

## Seasonal Considerations

- **Market peaks:** May through September. Sellers list high, competition is strongest, properties show best
- **Best negotiating window:** October through February. Lower buyer activity, sellers who listed in summer and didn't sell are more flexible. Snow reveals access and maintenance realities
- **Recommendation:** If timeline allows, identify properties during peak season, make offers during negotiating window
- **Weather verification:** Visit candidate properties in winter before committing. Year-round access claims must be verified in January, not July

## Infrastructure Checklist

For every shortlisted property, verify:
- **Internet:** Starlink availability confirmed (check coverage map). Fiber or cable availability is a bonus but not expected in rural Wyoming
- **Road access:** County-maintained road vs. private road. If private, verify maintenance agreement and winter plowing responsibility
- **Water:** Well capacity (GPM) or water rights documentation. Wyoming water law is prior appropriation -- verify priority date
- **Power:** Grid connection or off-grid capability. If off-grid, model solar/battery/generator costs into carrying cost

## Decision Rules

1. Every price must be verified against county assessor records or recent comparable sales. Listing prices are aspirational, not factual.
2. STR income models use conservative occupancy assumptions. Use 45-55% annual occupancy for Teton County, 30-40% for Sublette and Fremont. Never model at peak season rates year-round.
3. Properties that fail any of the five criteria are excluded. No exceptions, no "close enough." The criteria exist to prevent emotional purchases.
4. Flag any county-level STR regulation changes immediately. A property that is STR-capable today but restricted tomorrow changes the entire financial model.
5. Wyoming domicile timeline is non-negotiable. If property acquisition timeline slips past Q2 2027, escalate to Corey as P0.
6. If a property looks perfect but has a red flag (flood zone, conservation easement restricting use, contested water rights), present it with the red flag prominently displayed, not buried in footnotes.
