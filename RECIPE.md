# The Alloro Recipe

Read this first. Execute this always. Before any build, any output, any decision.

---

## 1. What We Make

Alloro is the scoreboard.

We show business owners the specific truth about their market that they cannot see without us. One finding. One dollar figure. One action. Before they pay us anything.

The category is Business Clarity. The enemy is opacity. The product is not a dashboard, not software, not reports. It is the moment a business owner sees something true about their business for the first time and knows exactly what to do.

## 2. Who We Make It For

Not a demographic. A felt state.

The person who built something real and accidentally became the manager of it. They are excellent at their craft. They are beginners at running a business. They have been promised results by agencies and consultants and have been disappointed, always. They are competitive. They are mildly anxious at 10pm on Sunday. They are skeptical of everyone who walks through the door with a pitch.

They want the life they thought they were buying when they built the business. Alloro gives that back.

## 3. Why They Pay

The patient pays the doctor for what they cannot diagnose themselves.
The business owner pays Alloro for what they cannot see themselves.

Same need. Same exchange. We see what they can't. We tell them what to do about it. They pay for that vision.

## 4. The Standard

Every output must do exactly two things:

**One:** Name the specific truth the owner could not see without Alloro. Which core need does it address: safety (am I losing ground?), status (am I winning?), belonging (is someone watching out for me?), or purpose (does this matter?).

**Two:** Attach the economic consequence if it goes unaddressed. At 30 days. At 90 days. At one year.

A finding without both is data.
A finding with both is intelligence.
Alloro only ships intelligence.

## 5. The Recipe

One finding. One dollar figure. One action. Never two. Never zero.

**Named.** Not "a competitor." Centerville Endodontics.
**Specific.** Not "revenue at risk." $18,000 per year.
**Plain English.** Not "referral velocity decline." Dr. Chen hasn't sent a case in 47 days.
**One action.** Not "consider outreach." Call Dr. Chen this week.
**No hedging.** Not "you may be losing ground." You dropped from #2 to #4.
**No jargon.** If the word could appear in a vendor pitch, remove it.

## 6. The Test

One question. Applied to every output before it ships.

**Would a business owner at 10pm, tired, mildly anxious, skeptical of everything, no time to read, understand this immediately and know exactly what to do next?**

If yes: ship it.
If no: simplify it. The problem is never that it needs more explanation. The problem is always that it isn't specific enough yet.

## 7. The One Rule

People are simple. They have a small set of needs. They are trying to avoid economic loss. Everything else exists to serve that simple truth, not to replace it.

If an output requires explanation before it creates clarity, it failed. Simplify it.
