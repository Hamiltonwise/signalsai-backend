# AAE Booth Script

Goal: Every doctor who stops gets a checkup run on their practice, sees something they did not know, and leaves their email. Target: under 90 seconds per demo.

---

## THE HOOK

Three options. Rotate based on energy and foot traffic.

**Option A: The Challenge**
"Do you know who your top competitor is and how many reviews they have? I can show you in 60 seconds."

Best when: the doctor is confident, competitive, making eye contact.

**Option B: The Mystery**
"I can tell you something about your business that you don't know. Free. Takes 60 seconds."

Best when: they are browsing, not in a rush, curious.

**Option C: The Silent Demo**
"What's your practice name?" [types it in] "Watch this."

Best when: the crowd is thick, energy is high, you need speed. Say less, let the screen do the work.

**If they hesitate:**
"It's free, it's fast, and I promise you'll learn something. Worst case, you wasted a minute."

---

## THE DEMO

Step-by-step. Every pause is intentional.

### Step 1: Type their practice name
Pull up getalloro.com/checkup on your phone. Type the practice name. Let them see you doing it live, not from a prepared list. This is real.

### Step 2: Let the scanning theater run (15 seconds)
Do NOT talk over this. The progress bar, the agent names appearing, the status updates. This is the show. Let it breathe. If they ask what's happening: "It's scanning your online presence, your reviews, your competitors, your search ranking. All at once."

### Step 3: Score reveals
The score appears. PAUSE. Do not explain. Let them react first.

If the score is low (under 60): "That number is your First Impression Score. It's what a new patient sees before they ever call your office."

If the score is high (75+): "That's strong. But look at this..." (move to Step 4)

If mid-range (60-74): "Not bad. But here's the part nobody tells you..."

### Step 4: The Oz Finding
This is the moment. Point to the insight they could not have known.

"Did you know your patients mention [specific theme] more than any of your competitors?"

Or: "Your patients are talking about [specific thing]. Nobody else in your area gets that. Did you know that?"

This is the "How did they know that?" moment. If this lands, the sale is made. Everything after is logistics.

### Step 5: The Competitor
"See this? That's [Competitor Name]. They have [N] reviews. You have [M]."

Do not editorialize. Let the numbers sit. If the gap is large, they will feel it. If the gap is small: "You're close. A few moves and you pass them."

### Step 6: The Improvement Plan
"These three things would take you from [current score] to [projected score]."

Point to each one. Keep it concrete: "This one is about review velocity. This one is your Google profile. This one is what patients say when they talk about you."

---

## THE CLOSE

The demo is done. Now capture the relationship.

**Primary close:**
"I'll send you this full report. What's the best email?"

Do not ask "Would you like me to send this?" Ask for the email directly. The value has already been delivered. The email is a formality.

**Secondary close (if they are engaged):**
"Want me to add your top competitor so we track them for you automatically? Every Monday you'll get an update."

**Tertiary close (if they are very engaged):**
"Here's the thing. This checkup is free, forever. If you want the agents watching your market every week, telling you the one thing to do every Monday morning, that's what Alloro does. But try the free version first. See what it finds."

**Hand them the card:**
QR code on the back points to getalloro.com/checkup. "Run it on a friend's practice. See what it says about them."

This turns every card into a second demo they run themselves.

---

## OBJECTION HANDLING

### "I already have Dental Intelligence / Jarvis / Pearl / [other tool]"
"Does it tell you what your patients say about you compared to your competitor? Run the checkup. Takes 60 seconds. If it tells you something they don't, you'll know."

Do not trash competitors. Let the checkup create the contrast.

### "I don't need marketing help"
"This isn't marketing. This is clarity. Do you know where your referrals actually come from? Do you know what patients say about you online vs. the practice down the street? That's not marketing. That's knowing your business."

### "How much does it cost?"
"The checkup is free. Forever. If you want Alloro watching your market every week and telling you the one thing to do every Monday, it's built for practices like yours. But let me show you what it finds first. No commitment."

Never lead with price. Always lead with value. If they push: "Plans start at what you'd pay a part-time front desk person for one day a week. Except this never calls in sick."

### "$2,000 seems high"
"What would it cost to hire someone to check your competitor's reviews every week, track your search ranking, monitor what patients are saying, and tell you the one thing to do every Monday morning? That's what the agents do. Twenty-four seven. And they get smarter every week."

If they still balk: "Start with the free checkup. See what it finds. If it's not useful, you've lost nothing."

### "I need to talk to my partner / office manager"
"Of course. Run the checkup right now so you have something to show them. It takes 60 seconds and the report is yours to keep."

Turn the objection into another demo.

### "I'll check it out later"
"Let me run it right now. If it's not interesting, I saved you the trouble of remembering. If it is, you'll want to show your team."

Speed kills procrastination.

### "Is this AI? I don't trust AI"
"The data is real. Reviews are real. Your Google ranking is real. The AI reads it faster than any human could and finds patterns you'd miss. But every number you see is sourced from public data. Nothing is made up."

## FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE

What happens after they walk away. All of this is automatic.

### Trigger
The moment they create an account or Corey enters their email with the checkup results, the 7-day trial sequence begins.

### Day 1: "Here's what we found"
Subject: Your First Impression Score is [X]
Body: The full checkup results. Score, competitor comparison, top findings. One line at the bottom: "Reply to this email if anything looks wrong. A real person reads every reply."

### Day 3: "The reply trap"
Subject: One quick question about [Practice Name]
Body: "Who's your top referral source? Reply with a name and we'll add them to your intelligence network. Takes 10 seconds."

Purpose: Get a reply. Any reply. A reply means engagement. A reply means their email client trusts us. A reply means the relationship is real.

### Day 5: "The referral ask"
Subject: Know someone who should see this?
Body: "You can run the checkup on any practice. If you know a colleague who'd want to see their score, send them this link: [personalized referral link]. If they sign up, you both get [reward]."

### Day 7: "The habit hook"
Subject: Your score this week
Body: First weekly score update. What changed, what moved, what to do next. This is the Monday email in miniature. If they open this, the habit has started.

### Day 14: (if no conversion)
Subject: [Practice Name] vs [Competitor Name] this week
Body: Weekly delta. Reviews gained, ranking movement, score change. Close with: "Want the full breakdown every Monday? Your trial is still active."

## BOOTH LOGISTICS

- Phone fully charged, backup battery in bag
- Chrome open to getalloro.com/checkup, logged in, ready
- WiFi tested at venue (and cellular fallback confirmed working)
- Demo mode enabled in case WiFi is unreliable
- Business cards with QR code in left pocket, pen in right
- Notebook for logging conversations (name, practice, what hooked them, objection, email captured Y/N)
- Water. You will talk for 8 hours straight.

## THE MINDSET

You are not selling software. You are showing someone a truth about their business that nobody else has told them. The software is the delivery mechanism. The value is the clarity.

Every doctor at AAE trained for years in a craft they love. They bought a practice to have freedom. Most of them discovered they had bought a second job instead. You are showing them that someone built something to give them that freedom back.

That is the pitch. Everything else is mechanics.
