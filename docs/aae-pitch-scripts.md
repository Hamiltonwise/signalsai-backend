# AAE Pitch Scripts

Four versions. Every version follows the same rules: pitch the finding, never pitch Alloro. The call to action is always "type your name." No claims about what Alloro does. Only claims about what you will see.

---

## 8-Second Version (The Morgan Test)

The doctor walks by. Glances at the banner. Corey has 8 seconds.

> "We already ran your competitors. Want to see who's winning?"

That is it. Do not explain. Do not qualify. The word "competitors" stops movement. The word "winning" creates urgency. If they slow down, you are in.

Alternate:

> "I can show you something about your market that nobody has told you. Takes 60 seconds."

---

## 2-Second Version (The QR Code Text)

Printed next to the QR code on the booth banner or table card:

**See where you rank.**

Below the code, smaller:

**Free. 60 seconds.**

No logo explanation. No product name. No tagline. The curiosity of "where you rank" plus the zero-risk framing of "free, 60 seconds" is enough. They scan without being asked because the question is about them, not about you.

---

## 30-Second Version (The Elevator)

The doctor stopped. They have 30 seconds of attention.

> "Every business owner has competitors they've never looked at closely. We scan your market, your reviews, your online presence, and your biggest competitors, then show you where you stand. Takes 60 seconds. All I need is your name."
>
> [Turn the screen toward them]
>
> "Type your name right here. You'll see your score before you finish your coffee."

The phone is already open to the checkup page. The keyboard is waiting. The action is typing, not deciding.

---

## 2-Minute Version (The Full Hook)

The doctor is standing at the booth. Two minutes before they move on.

> **[0:00]** "Let me show you something. Type your name right here."
>
> [Hand them the phone or tablet. Let them type. This matters. When they type their own name, they own the result.]
>
> **[0:15]** The scanning theater runs. Do NOT talk over it. The progress bar, the sources appearing, the status updates. Let it work. If they ask what is happening: "It is scanning your reviews, your competitors, your search rankings. All live."
>
> **[0:30]** The score appears. PAUSE. Let them react first.
>
> **[0:40]** "That number is your First Impression Score. It is what a new patient sees before they ever pick up the phone. Everything above 75 is strong. Everything below 60 means you are losing people before they walk in the door."
>
> **[0:55]** Point to the Oz finding. This is the moment.
>
> "See this? Your patients mention [specific theme] more than anyone else in your area. Did you know that?"
>
> Or: "See this competitor? They have [N] more reviews than you. But look at what your patients actually say. You are winning on [specific dimension] and you probably did not know it."
>
> **[1:15]** "These three things would move your score from [current] to [projected]. The first one takes five minutes."
>
> **[1:30]** "I will send you the full report. What is the best email?"
>
> Do not ask permission. The value was already delivered. The email is a receipt, not a request.
>
> **[1:45]** "One more thing. Run this on a colleague. See what it says about their practice. The link is on the card."
>
> Hand them the card. QR code on the back. Every card becomes a second demo they run themselves.

---

## Key Principles

1. **Never say "Alloro does X."** Say "You will see X."
2. **Never say "sign up."** Say "type your name."
3. **The Oz moment is the sale.** Everything before it is setup. Everything after it is logistics.
4. **Let the screen do the work.** The scanning theater, the score reveal, the competitor comparison. These are designed to create silence and reaction. Do not fill that silence with words.
5. **Every card is a second demo.** The QR code runs the checkup. Hand it to them. They will run it on a friend. That friend becomes a lead.
6. **Speed kills objections.** If they hesitate, run the checkup before they finish the sentence. Sixty seconds of seeing is worth sixty minutes of explaining.
